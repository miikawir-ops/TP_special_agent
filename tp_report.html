The ongoing Coca-Cola transfer pricing litigation, now framed as a "Post-Chevron Stress Test," highlights the enduring challenges in valuing high-value intangibles and the potential for increased judicial deference to tax authorities. This development significantly raises the stakes for multinational enterprises in defending their IP transfer pricing positions against a potentially more empowered IRS. In practice, companies must meticulously document IP valuations, incorporating robust economic analyses and anticipating a tougher stance from tax authorities during disputes.
