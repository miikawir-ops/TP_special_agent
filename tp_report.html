TaxGuru India Major ⚖ Court APAC
⚖ ITAT Deletes Transfer Pricing Adjustment as CCDs Are Not Call Options

The ITAT Mumbai deleted a transfer pricing adjustment, ruling that Compulsorily Convertible Debentures (CCDs) cannot be equated with call options. Consequently, the tribunal held that no option premium arose from their issuance, thus deleting the adjustment as no taxable income was generated from the intra-group financing.

TaxGuru India Major ⚖ Court APAC
⚖ ITAT Deletes CCD Interest Disallowance as Debentures Remain Debt Until Conversion

The ITAT Mumbai ruled that Compulsorily Convertible Debentures (CCDs) retain their character as debt until conversion into equity, deleting interest disallowance under Sections 36 and 37. While this determined the debt character, the tribunal remanded the transfer pricing benchmarking issue for the interest, indicating further scrutiny of its arm's length nature.

TaxGuru India Major ⚖ Court APAC
⚖ ITAT Upholds SEB Consumer Tariff for Section 80-IA Deduction on Captive Power Transfers

The ITAT Kolkata ruled that the arm's length price for electricity supplied by captive power plants, used for Section 80-IA deduction computations, should be benchmarked against tariffs charged by State Electricity Boards to industrial consumers. This decision upheld the deletion of a transfer pricing adjustment, referencing prior Supreme Court and Calcutta High Court rulings.

The ITAT Delhi held that foreign exchange gains derived from the realization of export proceeds for services rendered to associated enterprises are operating in nature for transfer pricing purposes.
