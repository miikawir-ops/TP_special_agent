TaxGuru India Major ⚖ Court APAC
⚖ TPO Cannot Value Management Fees at Nil Without Rejecting Evidence of Services Rendered

The ITAT Delhi ruled that a Transfer Pricing Officer cannot arbitrarily determine the arm's length price of intra-group management services at nil if evidence of services rendered exists. The Tribunal restored the assessee's benchmarking using the Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM), emphasizing that evidence must be rejected before a nil valuation is made.

TaxGuru India Major ⚖ Court APAC
⚖ ITAT Deletes TP Adjustment as TNMM Was Consistently Accepted in Earlier Years

The ITAT Ahmedabad deleted a transfer pricing adjustment, ruling that the Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM) remained the most appropriate method for the assessee. The Tribunal emphasized that there was no change in the assessee's functional profile and that earlier decisions had consistently accepted TNMM, overriding an adjustment based on the internal Cost Plus Method.

TaxGuru India Notable ⚖ Court APAC
⚖ ITAT Deletes Section 69C Addition as Source of Expenditure Was Fully Explained

The ITAT Mumbai held that Section 69C, pertaining to unexplained expenditure, cannot be invoked where expenditure is duly recorded in the books and its source is fully explained. The Tribunal consequently deleted additions related to royalty, commission, and technical service payments, affirming that these expenditures were adequately substantiated.
